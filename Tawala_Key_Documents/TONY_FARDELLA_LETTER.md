Subject: Tawala Project Archives — Request for Your Help

I hope this finds you well — it was great seeing you recently. I'm writing to ask for your help with something I've been meaning to do for a while: I'm looking to revive the Tawala platform, and I'm in the process of pulling together everything that survives from the original project.

- **The .NET source code** for the Designer desktop application — the .sln, .csproj, and .cs files that John built
- **Server-side code** — any ASP.NET pages, web services, or back-end files for build.tawala.com
- **PostgreSQL database schema** — table definitions, stored procedures, or any .sql scripts from Sergei's database work
- **Any server backups** from the live tawala.com or build.tawala.com servers, if you happen to have retained them
- **Anything else from the Tawala archive** that you think might be relevant — even partial files, old builds, or documentation are helpful

I also still have your old @tawala.com email address on file — I'm sending this to your current address, but wanted you to know I recognized your name from project correspondence.

As you know, I owned the company and retain all rights to the code, so there are no complications on that front — I'm simply trying to reassemble what exists before starting reconstruction work.

If you have an old hard drive, backup disk, or laptop from that era, it would genuinely be worth checking. Even incomplete materials give me a foundation to work from.

Please send whatever you find to [your email address], or reach out if a different transfer method would work better for larger files.

Your role in maintaining the Tawala archive made you the right person to contact, and I really appreciate any help you can offer.

Warm regards,
Doug Carlston
